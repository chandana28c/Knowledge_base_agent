EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK - HR POLICIES
Company: TechCorp Solutions
Last Updated: November 2024

========================================
1. LEAVE POLICY
========================================

1.1 Annual Leave
All full-time employees are entitled to 20 days of annual leave per calendar year. Part-time employees receive pro-rated leave based on their working hours. Annual leave must be approved by the immediate supervisor at least 5 working days in advance.

1.2 Sick Leave
Employees can take up to 12 days of sick leave per year with proper medical documentation. A doctor's certificate is required for sick leave exceeding 2 consecutive days. Sick leave does not carry forward to the next year.

1.3 Casual Leave
7 days of casual leave are provided per year for personal matters. Casual leave can be taken without prior approval for urgent situations, but employees must inform their manager as soon as possible.

1.4 Maternity Leave
Female employees are entitled to 180 days (approximately 6 months) of paid maternity leave. This can be availed before or after childbirth as per the employee's preference and medical advice.

1.5 Paternity Leave
Male employees are entitled to 10 days of paternity leave within 6 months of the child's birth.

========================================
2. WORK HOURS AND ATTENDANCE
========================================

2.1 Standard Work Hours
The standard working hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday, with a one-hour lunch break. Employees are expected to work 40 hours per week.

2.2 Flexible Work Policy
Employees may request flexible work arrangements, including work-from-home options, subject to manager approval and business requirements. Remote work requires a stable internet connection and dedicated workspace.

2.3 Attendance Tracking
All employees must log their attendance through the company's HRMS portal. Late arrivals (after 9:15 AM) must be reported to the manager. Consistent tardiness may result in disciplinary action.

========================================
3. COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS
========================================

3.1 Salary Structure
Salaries are paid on the last working day of each month via direct bank transfer. The salary structure includes basic pay, house rent allowance (HRA), and other applicable allowances as per company policy.

3.2 Performance Bonus
Annual performance bonuses are awarded based on individual performance reviews and company profitability. Bonuses typically range from 10% to 20% of annual salary for high performers.

3.3 Health Insurance
All employees and their immediate family members (spouse and up to 2 children) are covered under the company's group health insurance policy. Coverage includes hospitalization, pre and post-hospitalization expenses up to Rs. 5 lakhs per year.

3.4 Provident Fund
The company contributes 12% of the basic salary to the Employee Provident Fund (EPF) as per government regulations. Employees also contribute an equal amount.

3.5 Professional Development
Employees can access up to Rs. 50,000 per year for professional development courses, certifications, and conference attendance, subject to manager approval and relevance to job role.

========================================
4. CODE OF CONDUCT
========================================

4.1 Professional Behavior
All employees are expected to maintain professional conduct in the workplace. This includes treating colleagues with respect, maintaining confidentiality, and adhering to company values.

4.2 Dress Code
Business casual attire is expected on regular working days. Formal business attire is required for client meetings and important business events.

4.3 Workplace Safety
Employees must follow all safety protocols and immediately report any safety hazards to the facilities team. Safety training is mandatory for all new employees.

========================================
5. GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
========================================

5.1 Reporting Issues
Employees can report grievances, complaints, or concerns to their immediate supervisor or the HR department. All complaints are treated confidentially and investigated promptly.

5.2 Escalation Process
If issues are not resolved at the supervisor level, employees can escalate to the department head and subsequently to HR. An anonymous hotline is also available for serious concerns.

========================================
6. TERMINATION AND EXIT PROCESS
========================================

6.1 Resignation
Employees wishing to resign must provide 30 days written notice (60 days for senior positions). The notice period can be negotiated based on business needs.

6.2 Exit Formalities
Exiting employees must complete all exit formalities including knowledge transfer, return of company property, and exit interview with HR. Final settlement is processed within 30 days of the last working day.
